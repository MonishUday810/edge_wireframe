# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Three distinct user types, each with a different relationship to the tool:

- **Classroom teachers** (primary): Using the tool before and during lessons on a laptop or tablet. Often time-pressured. Needs to find information fast, trust the structure, and not be distracted by the interface. This is the most demanding use case.
- **School administrators / heads of department**: Reviewing lesson plans and block progress, approving content, tracking curriculum coverage. Less time-pressured but expects a professional, credible surface.
- **Parents**: Reviewing homework, parent tips, and what their child is covering. Occasional users; should feel approachable and clear without feeling like a student app.

## Product Purpose

SuperTeacherEDGE is a structured curriculum delivery platform for primary schools. It gives teachers a single place to open, read, and execute lesson plans that follow the 5A structure (Aim, Action, Analysis, Application, Assessment). It also gives administrators visibility over curriculum coverage, and gives parents a window into what their child is learning.

Success looks like: a teacher opening the lesson plan page 30 seconds before class and knowing exactly what to do.

## Brand Personality

Modern, energetic, warm, classy. The confidence of a well-designed professional tool, the warmth of something made by people who care about education, and enough visual energy to feel like an upgrade to the classroom rather than a bureaucratic necessity.

Voice: direct, purposeful, never patronising. Talks to teachers as professionals.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS cream**: the beige-white-rounded-corners dashboard that every EdTech startup ships. Forgettable, interchangeable, no soul.
- **Corporate LMS**: Moodle, Blackboard, Google Classroom in its default state. Grey, dense, zero delight, zero pride of ownership.
- Not a student app. No gamification, no Duolingo energy, nothing that would make a teacher feel like they're being treated as a learner themselves.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed to action.** Every screen should surface the most critical information within 3 seconds of opening. Teachers don't browse — they scan and act.
2. **Earned authority.** The interface should feel like a well-designed professional tool that a good teacher would be proud to use in front of a class. Credibility through craft, not decoration.
3. **Structure that breathes.** The 5A lesson structure is rigid by design; the interface should make that structure feel clear and navigable, not claustrophobic. White space and hierarchy do the work.
4. **Warmth without informality.** The product serves human relationships — teacher to student, teacher to parent. The interface should carry that warmth without sliding into casual or consumer-app territory.
5. **Context switching is expensive.** Teachers, admins, and parents are always operating in a different mental mode. The interface should reduce cognitive load, not add to it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort accessibility. Aim for WCAG 2.1 AA compliance on contrast and keyboard navigation. No formal audit requirement, but the product is used in schools where accessibility matters and should not be an afterthought.
